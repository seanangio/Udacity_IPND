All About Haikus

An old silent pond
A frog jumps into the pond
splash! Silence again.
-Basho Matsuo

Light of the moon
Moves west, flowers' shadows
Creep eastward.
-Yosa Buson

O snail
Climb Mount Fuji,
But slowly, slowly!
-Kobayaski Issa

Over the wintry
forest, winds howl in rage
with no leaves to blow.
-Natsume Soseki

Introduction

Haiku is a short form of Japanese poetry traditionally composed of seventeen syllables in lines of five, seven and five. Listen to the audio file for correct pronunciation of the word haiku.

During the Heian period of Japanese culture (700-1100), it was a social requirement to be able to instantly recognize, appreciate and recite Japanese and Chinese poetry. It was around this period that short forms of poetry (tanka) grew in popularity over long forms of poetry (choka). The rigid lifestyles of the time carried over into art; every poem had to have a specific form. The approved form was the 5-7-5 triplet followed by a couplet of seven syllables (this was the Japanese equivalent to the iambic pentameter of Shakespeare's England) (Source).

One of the most famous haikus comes from Basho titled "old pond". In translation, it reads:

old pond
a frog leaps in
water's sound
